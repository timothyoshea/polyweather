❓ Help & Guide

How PolyWeather Works
PolyWeather scans Polymarket weather temperature markets every 10 minutes. It compares multi-model weather forecasts against market prices to find edges, then opens trades automatically based on your portfolio rules.

The cycle: Scan markets → Forecast temperatures → Find edges → Open trades → Resolve when market settles
Bet Types Explained
Sure Bet — High probability (>92%), low price. The forecast is very confident the outcome will happen, and the market hasn't priced it in yet. Low risk, low return.

Edge Bet — Moderate probability with a significant edge (>8%) over market price. Higher risk, higher potential return. These rely on forecast accuracy.

Safe NO — Betting that something WON'T happen. The forecast says probability is very high (>97%), so the NO side is very cheap. You buy NO at 3-10 cents and collect $1 if the event doesn't happen.
Paper vs Live Portfolios
Paper portfolios simulate trades without real money. Use these to test strategies and build confidence.

Live portfolios place real orders on Polymarket using USDC.e. Create a live portfolio from Settings by clicking "Create Live Portfolio" — this walks you through confirming every setting before going live.

Paper and live portfolios are completely separate. Your paper portfolios continue tracking independently.
Understanding Trading Rules
Allowed Bet Types / Sides / Bands — Filter which trades the portfolio takes. E.g. NO-only portfolios only bet against outcomes.

Capital Management — Controls risk. Max single trade caps position size. Max utilization prevents deploying all capital at once. City exposure limits prevent concentration in one market.

Trading Hours — Restrict when trades can be placed (UTC). Use allowed windows to trade only during active market hours, or blackout windows to avoid specific times.
Key Metrics
Edge — Difference between our forecast probability and market price. Higher = more potential profit.

Confidence — How much agreement there is between weather models. Higher = more reliable forecast.

EV/Dollar — Expected value per dollar invested. Combines edge and price to show return efficiency.

Half Kelly — Suggested position size using half-Kelly criterion. Balances growth vs risk.
Dashboard Pages
Dashboard — Open trades, current opportunities, and quick stats for the selected portfolio.

P&L — Profit & loss charts, cumulative returns, and closed trade history with date filtering.

Analysis — Breakdowns by city, bet type, side, and more. Statistical significance testing. AI-powered insights.

Compare — Side-by-side performance comparison across all portfolios.

Settings — Scanner controls, portfolio management, trading rules, and this help guide.

What PolyWeather Does

  • Scans Polymarket weather temperature prediction markets every 10 minutes
  • Compares market prices against multi-model weather forecasts (Open-Meteo, NOAA)
  • Identifies pricing edges and opens paper trades automatically

Bet Types

  • Sure Bets — High probability (>92%), low price — nearly certain outcomes
  • Edge Bets — Moderate probability with significant edge over market price
  • Safe NOs — High-probability NO bets where the market is overpricing YES

Portfolio System

  • Multiple portfolios with separate strategies and capital management
  • Each portfolio can have its own allowed sides, bet types, blocked cities
  • Capital management enforces position sizing, utilization limits, and correlated exposure caps

Key Pages

  • Dashboard — Live trades table, scan controls (in Settings)
  • P&L — Profit/loss tracking with date filters and capital status
  • Analysis — AI-powered trade analysis with Claude Opus, breakdowns, pattern detection
  • Compare — Side-by-side portfolio performance comparison
  • Settings — Scanner tools, portfolio creation, strategy editor

Understanding the Trade Table

  • Edge — How much better our forecast is vs market price (higher = better)
  • Confidence — Model agreement score (higher = more reliable)
  • Half-Kelly — Suggested position size as % of bankroll
  • Entry Price — VWAP across filled book levels
  • Mark-to-Market ($) — Unrealized P&L based on current market price
